About Me

I'm Luciano Clementi, and I've always been drawn to the process of creating something from nothing. What started as a personal interest has grown into a committed pursuit. I realised how much I enjoyed influencing emotion, rhythm, and story through video editing which has led me into front and back end development and interactive design.

I'm currently studying Interactive Media Design at Fanshawe College, working toward graduating in April 2026. During this chapter of my career I have developed strong skills accross both visual and technical practices, allowing me to create comfortably between motion, design, and code. I enjoy tackling creative-direction challenges and shaping projects that are purposeful, enaging, and visually appealing.

My goal is to take on remote friendly work that requires motion and front end development, collaborating with teams who value the creative process and enjoy working together to build impactful content.

Cartoon Bumper

Design Animation Video Editing
View

3D designed Cartoon bumper clip.

Overview

A short, 3D animated bumper created to introduce an episode of a cartoon show. I designed and animated the entire scene using Cinema 4D. The goal was to make a playful and fun scene that promotes the show.

Programs used

  • Cinema 4D / Redshift
  • Adobe After Effects
  • Adobe Premiere Pro

Problems faced and solved

The main challenge was inconsistent render settings that slowed down my production time. My first render exported with the wrong resolution and colour palette, requiring me to render the entire bumper a second time. To avoid this happening again, I set up and saved render presets linked to specific styles and goals.

Custom Earbuds

Front-end Back-end Design Animation Video Editing
View

Promotional website for a unique 3D earbud design.

Overview

I designed a 3D model of a pair of earbuds and their charging case using Cinema 4D and Redshift, then built a promotional webpage to showcase the product. The page highlights key features through an interactive model viewer, an X-ray comparison slider, and a series of promotional images.

Programs used

  • Cinema 4D / Redshift
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • HTML5
  • CSS3
  • JavaScript
  • Model Viewer

Problems faced and solved

The main issue I faced was that the 3D model was not centered in the Cinema 4D workspace, which made it difficult to accurately place the informational hotspots in the browser. After identifying the problem, I repositioned the model correctly, re-exported the .glb file, and the hotspots aligned instantly. This fix ensured that each feature callout appeared exactly where intended on the final interactive model.

Burple Re-brand

Front-end Back-end Design Animation Video Editing
View

Promotional website for a soda rebrand.

Overview

I created a promotional rebrand for Burple, designing a new 3D can concept in Cinema 4D as well as a set of visuals used for the video scenes. The project includes a promotional website that presents a short animated promo, marketing images, and the different products available.

Programs used

  • Cinema 4D / Redshift
  • Adobe After Effects
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • HTML5
  • CSS3
  • JavaScript

Problems faced and solved

The biggest challenge I faced was achieving a realistic look in the 3D scenes. My early renders felt flat and artificial, so I researched how to improve my lighting setups, address surface imperfections, and refine texturing. By adjusting the light rig, adding subtle roughness variations, and tuning material properties, the final visuals became far more believable and engaging.
